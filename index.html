About Me

A freshly bought loaf of Bread has 3 lifespans in our house;

  1. The ‘it-never-existed’ lifespan,
  2. The ‘Consumed-before-going-bad’ lifespan, and finally,
  3. The ‘we-are-feeling-generous-and-would-like-to-share-with-the-local-fungi’ lifespan.

Depending on the environmental factors in the fridge, the bread assumes one of the lifespans. In the presence of eggs, the bread takes up the first. In the absence of eggs but in the presence of Peanut Butter, the bread survives a decent life while not going stale. In the absence of both eggs and peanut butter, the bread defaults to the third lifespan.

That is me in a nutshell. I observe the exquisite simplicity found in the commonplace, appreciating the details that others might dismiss.
